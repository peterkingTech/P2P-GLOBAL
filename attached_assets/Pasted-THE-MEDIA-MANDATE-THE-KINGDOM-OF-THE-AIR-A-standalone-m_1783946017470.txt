MA-L01 – The Human Will: Power and Peril ☐
DEFINITION: THE HUMAN WILL
What Is It?
The will is the faculty of choice — the capacity to decide and act independently. It is, in this teaching, the single most powerful force on earth: so powerful that even God does not override it, because the very nature of a will requires self-control rather than external control.
Why Did God Give Humanity a Will?
The will is the agency through which God intended to administrate His kingdom on earth. He rules through willing agents, not apart from them — He gave dominion to man (Genesis 1:26-28) and governs through human choice rather than force.
Why Is the Will Dangerous?
A will is both a precious and dangerous gift. Because it is real freedom, it gives man the ability to choose even against the will of the One who gave it. "I have set before you life and death, blessings and curses. Now choose life" (Deuteronomy 30:19) — God pleads for the right choice but does not compel it.
To Whom Was the Will Given?
To every human being without exception. It is not earned or granted selectively — it is part of being made in God's image.
NOTE: Because God will not violate the will, whoever controls what information reaches it effectively controls the direction of its choices. That's the doorway the enemy exploits (see Lesson 4).

MA-L02 – The Mind: Conscious, Subconscious, and the Heart ☐
DEFINITION: THE CONSCIOUS AND SUBCONSCIOUS MIND
What Is It?
The will resides in both the conscious and subconscious mind. The conscious mind processes immediate thought; the subconscious — what Scripture calls "the heart" — is the deeper seat of control that actually governs a person's settled beliefs and behavior.
Why Does the Subconscious ("the Heart") Matter So Much?
"As he thinks in his heart, so is he" (Proverbs 23:7). What has been deposited in the subconscious, not merely what a person consciously affirms, determines who they actually are. The conscious mind feeds the subconscious; constant repetition "downloads" that information onto the subconscious "hard drive."
Why Is Repetition So Powerful?
Whatever is repeated — words, images, ideas — eventually moves from conscious awareness into subconscious conviction, where it operates automatically, often without further conscious decision.
To Whom Does This Apply?
To every person constantly, whether aware of it or not. Media, conversation, habit, and self-talk are all continuously downloading information into the subconscious — for good or harm.
NOTE: Whoever controls the information entering the subconscious controls the man. Guarding what gets repeated into your heart is a matter of guarding your future.

MA-L03 – The Soul: The Battleground ☐
DEFINITION: THE SOUL
What Is It?
The soul is an integration of the mind, will, and emotions. It functions as the "media" or mediator between the human spirit and the physical body — receiving, translating, and passing information between the two.
Why Is the Soul Described as a Mediator?
The soul receives data from the five senses (through the body) and deposits it toward the spirit; conversely, the spirit reveals its desires through the soul to the body. Every decision passes through this soul-mediation.
Why Does This Produce a "Mental Battle"?
When the spirit (influenced by God) and the body (influenced by the senses and the world) send conflicting information into the soul, a struggle results. "For the flesh desires what is contrary to the Spirit, and the Spirit what is contrary to the flesh" (Galatians 5:17). "May your whole spirit, soul and body be kept blameless" (1 Thessalonians 5:23) shows all three as distinct yet meant to work in harmony.
To Whom Does This Battle Belong?
To every believer navigating competing inputs daily. The soul doesn't get to opt out of mediating — it must choose, moment by moment, whose voice it passes to the will.
NOTE: Because the soul is a mediator, not a source, whoever controls what reaches the soul controls what gets passed to the will — and ultimately to action.

MA-L04 – Media and the Kingdom of the Air ☐
DEFINITION: MEDIA AND THE KINGDOM OF THE AIR
What Is It?
"Media" is derived from "medium," meaning to stand between or to mediate. Whoever controls the medium controls the message — its quality, framing, and meaning. Ephesians 2 describes Satan as "the ruler of the kingdom of the air, the spirit who is now at work in those who are disobedient" (Ephesians 2:2).
Why Is the Air More Powerful Than the Earth?
The air contains the waves — radio, television, brain waves — that carry messages and shape a nation's thinking. Territory on earth is limited by geography; the airwaves are not.
What Is the Enemy's Goal?
Satan's scheme is to use the airwaves to establish "principalities" in the minds of people — defined as precepts, principles, laws, and ideas — in order to control their future. A principality isn't a demon in a location; it's an idea entrenched in a culture's thinking.
To Whom Is This Directed?
To every mind exposed to media — in a world saturated with television, internet, and music, effectively everyone. The battle for the nations is fought first in the air, over what ideas get repeated into the collective subconscious.
NOTE: If the mediator (the media) is corrupt, the message reaching the soul and subconscious is distorted — regardless of how true the original truth may be.

MA-L05 – Kingdom Strategy: Occupying the Airwaves ☐
DEFINITION: THE STRATEGY FOR KINGDOM VICTORY
What Is It?
The Word (Logos — the expression of a thought) is God's chosen medium: God used the medium of the Word to create and sustain the world (John 1:1-3). The Church's strategy for victory is to use that same medium to reclaim the airwaves for the Kingdom.
Why Must the "Hard Drive" Be Cleaned?
To break old habits or sinful thinking already downloaded into the subconscious, a person must download new information to replace it. The Holy Spirit acts as God's "software," and the Word of God is the material used to drown out corrupt data. "Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind" (Romans 12:2).
What Is the Church's Mandate?
"Occupy till I come" (Luke 19:13, KJV). This includes taking control of the airwaves — media, internet, music, television — to influence the national psyche with Kingdom principles rather than ceding that territory by default.
To Whom Is This Mandate Given?
To the whole Church, not a specialized few. Every believer who consumes or produces media participates in either occupying the air for the Kingdom or surrendering it to another ruler.
NOTE: "Clearing the air" of demonic strongholds and principalities is both a present mandate and a preparation — believers are also destined for a final meeting with Christ "in the air" (1 Thessalonians 4:17).

TEACHING OUTLINE — THE MEDIA MANDATE: THE KINGDOM OF THE AIR
Opening Illustration: The Radio Tower
A broadcast tower never has to enter your house — it just has to control the frequency, and the message reaches everyone tuned in. This is exactly how the mind works: whoever controls the frequency of repeated information controls what gets downloaded into the heart.
Session MA-A: How You Are Wired (Lessons 1-3)
The will is the seat of choice God will not override. The subconscious ("the heart") is shaped by repetition, not intention. The soul mediates between spirit and body, and the "mental battle" is the natural result of that mediation.
Session MA-B: The War for the Air (Lessons 4-5)
Media is a mediator, and Satan is called "ruler of the kingdom of the air" who uses airwaves to plant principalities in the mind. The Church's counter-strategy is the Word (Logos) and the Holy Spirit, deployed through the mandate to "occupy" — including the airwaves themselves.

DISCUSSION QUESTIONS — THE MEDIA MANDATE

Will & Choice: Where have you felt the weight of your own freedom to choose, even against what you know is right?
Repetition: What have you repeated into your own subconscious lately — on purpose or not — that you'd want to "un-download"?
The Soul's Battle: Describe a recent moment when your senses/body and your spirit gave conflicting signals. Which one won?
Media & Principalities: What "principality" (idea, precept, belief) do you think has been downloaded into your generation through media?
Occupying: What's one piece of media — a show, platform, habit — where you could be "occupying" for the Kingdom instead of just consuming?
